Tutti-frutti Cake

What is Tutti-frutti?

Tutti-frutti is candied fruit, typically watermelon, papaya, raisins, currants, etc. You can make it by boiling fruit of your choice in a simple sugar syrup. Tutti-frutti first originated in Italy as a flavor for ice cream with candied fruits, nuts, and occasionally dried fruits. Tutti-frutti traces back to a receipt from 1860 with recipes for/using it in the late 19th century. Tutti-frutti also refers to a flavor with it being one of the first gum flavors sold in a New York vending machine. While this term clearly has multiple interpretations, the tutti-frutti I use for this cake is candied papaya that gets artificially colored.

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cup + 1 tbsp Flour
  • A pinch of Salt
  • 1/2 tsp Baking Soda
  • 1 1/4 tsp Baking Powder
  • 3/4 cup Sugar
  • 1/2 cup Tutti-frutti
  • 1/2 cup Melted Butter
  • 1 cup Milk
  • 1 1/4 tbsp Vinegar or Lemon Juice
  • 1 tbsp Vanilla Extract
  • 1/3 cup Sliced Almonds or any nut of choice (optional)

Recipe

  1. Preheat oven to 340 degrees Fahrenheit
  2. Cover tutti-frutti with 1 tbsp flour and mix well to prevent sticking
  3. Sift together 1 ½ cup flour, ½ tsp baking soda, 1 ¼ tsp baking powder, and a pinch of salt into a large bowl
  4. Sift again into a mixing bowl and make a well in the center
  5. Add 1 cup room temperature milk to a different bowl
  6. Add 1 ¼ tbsp vinegar or lemon juice to the milk
  7. Add 1 cup sugar to the milk
  8. Add 1 tbsp vanilla extract to the milk
  9. Melt ½ cup of butter and add to the mixture and mix well
  10. Add wet mixture to the center of the well and gently mix with spatula or whisk
  11. Add coated tutti-frutti and nuts and gently mix in
  12. Add the batter to a greased 4x8 loaf tin and tap on the surface to remove air bubbles
  13. Bake in the oven for around 45 minutes, check with a toothpick
